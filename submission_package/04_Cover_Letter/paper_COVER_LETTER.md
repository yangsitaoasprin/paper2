Dear Editor,

Please consider our manuscript, "Task-Aligned Complexity in Small-Sample Multi-Enzyme CYP450 Prediction: A Benchmark-Guided Analysis", for publication as a Research Article in the *Journal of Chemical Information and Modeling*.

This study addresses a practical methodological question in CYP450 substrate prediction: when a benchmark is small, enzyme-conditional, and structurally heterogeneous, which forms of model complexity are genuinely justified? Rather than proposing another nominally stronger architecture, we organize the manuscript as a controlled benchmark analysis on a released CYP450 pair benchmark containing 15,005 drug-enzyme pairs across six major human isoforms. The study compares strong lightweight baselines, explicit enzyme-aware routes, later engineering additions, targeted enzyme-side refinement, and scaffold-based robustness evaluation under one coordinated experimental sequence.

The main findings are threefold. First, enzyme context materially improves performance over a structure-only lower bound, confirming that this is a genuinely conditional drug-enzyme prediction task. Second, under the released benchmark split, a compact Morgan-CNN route remains the strongest benchmark-efficient baseline, and later engineering variants do not deliver consistent pooled gains. Third, under scaffold split, the leading group shifts toward more explicitly conditioned enzyme-aware routes, showing that the preferred level of complexity is evaluation-regime dependent rather than universally maximal or minimal. We believe this benchmark-guided perspective is relevant to the JCIM readership because it connects molecular machine learning practice with benchmark design, model selection, and realistic evidence standards in drug-metabolism prediction.

This manuscript is original, has not been published previously, and is not under consideration for publication elsewhere. All authors have approved the manuscript and agree with its submission. We have no conflicts of interest to declare beyond those stated in the manuscript.
